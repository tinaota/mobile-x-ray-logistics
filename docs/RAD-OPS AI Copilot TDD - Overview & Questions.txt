RAD-OPS AI CO-PILOT — TDD OVERVIEW & REVIEW QUESTIONS
Source document: RAD-OPS AI Copilot TDD V1.docx (Doc ID RAD-OPS-TDD-001, v0.2 DRAFT, dated July 7, 2026, prepared by Kenneth Rubio)
Prepared for: non-technical review ahead of Solution Architect / stakeholder sign-off

====================================================
WHAT THIS DOCUMENT ACTUALLY IS
====================================================
It's a blueprint document for adding an AI assistant ("AI Co-Pilot") into the RAD-OPS platform.
It's written for three audiences at once: sales will turn parts of it into a contract (Statement
of Work), developers will turn other parts into a build spec, and a Solution Architect needs to
approve the technical design before any of that happens. Right now it's still a draft — nothing
has been built or signed off yet.

====================================================
WHAT THE AI CO-PILOT ACTUALLY DOES
====================================================
Think of it as a smart assistant that sits inside the existing software, not a general chatbot
like ChatGPT. The key difference: every answer it gives is based on real, live data — actual
orders, technicians, specimens, invoices — pulled fresh each time someone asks it something. It's
specifically designed to never make things up about a patient or an order, which matters a lot in
healthcare.

It comes in three "modes" depending on who's using it:
  - Dispatcher mode  — helps decide who covers an urgent (STAT) job before a specimen expires,
                        flags coverage gaps
  - Billing mode     — checks claims for missing codes or denial risk BEFORE submission, and
                        flags unbilled revenue sitting around
  - Field-Tech mode  — helps technicians in the field with "what do I do next" questions and
                        chain-of-custody steps

IMPORTANT LIMIT FOR V1: it only recommends — it can't actually assign an order or fix an invoice
itself. A human still has to click the button.

====================================================
THE BUSINESS CASE (Section 3)
====================================================
The problem it's solving: staff currently hold critical, time-pressured decisions in their heads
(which tech covers this STAT order, will this claim get denied). The pitch is fewer lost
specimens, fewer denied claims, less wasted time, and easier onboarding for new field techs.

The ROI numbers in the document (~$138,000/year illustrative benefit) are ENTIRELY MADE-UP
PLACEHOLDER NUMBERS to show how the math would work — not this company's actual numbers. Nobody
has plugged in real volumes yet.

====================================================
TIMELINE & COST
====================================================
Roughly a 12-week build, broken into 6 phases:
  WP1 Foundations        -> environment setup, login/role gate, database schema, demo mode
  WP2 Streaming Core     -> the live chat engine itself
  WP3 Context Engine     -> connecting it to real, live operational data
  WP4 Workspace UI       -> building the actual screens
  WP5 Hardening & UAT    -> security testing
  WP6 Deploy & Handover  -> going live, training, handoff

Running the AI has an ongoing cost (Anthropic's API usage) that the document says is billed to
the client, but it does not give a number.

====================================================
SECURITY
====================================================
Login-gated, role-restricted (only "copilot" and "admin" roles can access it), secrets kept
server-side, and the AI is explicitly instructed never to invent patient health information.
Chat transcripts get saved to a database table for record-keeping.

====================================================
THREE SOLID QUESTIONS TO RAISE AT THE REVIEW
====================================================

1) COST & ROI — "Show me our numbers, not example numbers"
   Ask: "Section 3.5's ROI model looks solid as a framework, but every input — expired
   specimens, denial rate, claim volume — is a placeholder. Before we commit, I want our
   actual 12-month volumes run through this model."
   Follow-up: "The document also says we're billed for Anthropic API usage (Section 6.1) but
   gives no estimate. What's the expected monthly range once this is live at our volume?"
   Why it matters: you're being asked to evaluate a business case built on invented numbers —
   reasonable to ask for the real one before sign-off.

2) PATIENT DATA STORAGE — "This is still an open question in your own document"
   Ask: "Section 17, Q2 says transcript retention, encryption, and audit logging for stored
   chat messages — which may reference patient info — are still undecided. That's a compliance
   gap, not a technical detail. Who owns closing that before this goes anywhere near
   production?"
   Follow-up: "Is that decision happening before the 12-week build starts, or is it being
   deferred into Work Package 1 or 5? I'd want it resolved before, not during."
   Why it matters: a HIPAA-adjacent decision is currently unowned — fair to push on as a
   non-technical stakeholder.

3) "RECOMMENDS, DOESN'T ACT" — sanity-check the savings math
   Ask: "Section 4.2 is explicit that v1 never takes action — no auto-assigning orders, no
   auto-fixing invoices. A person still does that step by hand every time. Does the $138,000
   illustrative benefit assume that manual step, or does it assume the AI just handles it?"
   Why it matters: easy detail to gloss over in a pitch, and it directly affects whether the
   savings estimate is realistic for year one.

OPTIONAL CLOSING LINE, if you want to sound sharp:
   "This reads like a well-structured draft — I just want the placeholder numbers replaced
   with ours, and the PHI storage question closed, before it goes to SOW."
